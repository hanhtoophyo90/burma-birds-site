What your support helps build

  • • Better species pages with photos, records, habitats, and references
  • • Identify and observation workflows that feed the species catalog
  • • Document storehouse for checklists, papers, and field reports
  • • Future map layers, hotspot tracking, and conservation dashboards
  • • Educational resources for students and community learners

Why this matters

A shared bird portal can connect field workers, students, teachers, and conservationists in one place. It turns scattered information into a searchable knowledge base for species identification, observation, and biodiversity education.

Thank you for helping protect Burma’s biodiversity and for supporting open, community-driven science.
